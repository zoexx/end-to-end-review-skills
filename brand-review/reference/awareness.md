# Awareness (认知) — do they know you?

The top of the funnel. Awareness is *unprompted recall and consideration* within your
category — not impressions. Impressions are reach you paid for; awareness is memory you
earned. The diagnostic question: **of the people who could buy in your category, how many
think of you?**

Score this against the *category*, never against the whole internet.

## Metrics

### Share of Search — the best single proxy for brand strength
The share of category search volume that is *your brand* vs all brands in the category.
`your brand search volume ÷ (sum of all competitor brand search volumes)`.

- **Source:** Google Trends (compare your brand term vs top competitors), or Search Console
  + a keyword tool for absolute volumes. Track the *trend*, not just the level.
- **Why it matters:** Share of search leads market share by 6–18 months — it's a forward
  indicator. Rising = you're winning mindshare; falling = a competitor is, even if revenue
  hasn't moved yet.
- **Benchmark:** there's no universal number — compare to direct competitors and to your own
  trend. A category leader often holds 30–60% share of search; a challenger climbing from
  5% → 12% is a strong signal.

### Brand Awareness (aided & unaided)
Survey-measured: *unaided* ("name brands that do X") and *aided* ("which of these have you
heard of?").

- **Source:** brand-tracking survey, panel study, or a quick audience poll. Pre/post a
  campaign = brand lift.
- **Benchmark:** unaided awareness in a niche B2B category might be 5–15%; a consumer
  category leader 60%+. The number that matters is the *delta over time*.

### Direct Traffic
Sessions arriving by typing the URL / bookmark / untracked — people who already know you.

- **Source:** GA4 "Direct" channel (caveat: dark social and stripped UTMs inflate it).
- **Read:** a healthy, growing direct share means awareness is compounding. Direct that's
  ~flat while paid traffic climbs = you're renting attention, not building memory.

### Organic Mention
Unpaid mentions of the brand across social, press, forums, communities — volume + reach.

- **Source:** social listening; the **`last30days`** skill for fresh cross-platform mention
  volume; press/Google Alerts. Track volume *and* whether mentions are in the right context.
- **Read:** rising organic mention is awareness working for free. Near-zero mention with high
  ad spend means nothing is sticking.

## Diagnosing low Awareness

- **Low share of search, low everything** → you have a *reach* problem (pre-fit) or a
  *memorability* problem (people see you and forget). Distinguish: if paid impressions are
  high but direct/branded search is flat, it's memorability — the creative/positioning isn't
  distinctive. Fix positioning before buying more reach.
- **High awareness, low everything downstream** → awareness isn't the constraint; don't
  spend here. The leak is Relevance or below. (See the cross-stage tree in
  `scoring-and-funnel.md`.)
- **Awareness rising, revenue flat** → normal lag (share of search leads). Stay the course
  if the downstream stages are healthy.

## Levers (only if Awareness is the bottleneck)

- **Distinctive brand assets** — a consistent name, logo, color, and tagline used everywhere,
  so each impression compounds the same memory instead of starting over.
- **Category entry points** — show up where buyers first feel the need, in the language they
  use, so you're the brand recalled at the moment of need.
- **Earned > paid for memory** — PR, partnerships, community, and shareable content build
  recall that doesn't disappear when the ad budget does.

## Common ways the number lies

- **Impressions ≠ awareness.** A billion impressions nobody remembers is worth zero recall.
- **Direct traffic is polluted** by dark social and lost UTMs — don't over-read small moves.
- **Branded search can be bought** — strip out branded paid clicks before calling it organic
  awareness.
