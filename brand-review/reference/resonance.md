# Resonance (共鸣) — do they *feel* it?

Relevance is rational ("this fits my need"); Resonance is emotional ("this gets me / I want
others to see this"). It's the difference between a click and a memory, a customer and a fan.
Resonance is what makes a brand *spread* — it's the bridge between Conversion (value to you)
and Advocacy (others brought in). The question: **does engaging with the brand create enough
feeling that people act on it — comment, save, share, make something?**

## Metrics

### Engagement Rate
Interactions (likes, comments, saves, shares, clicks) ÷ reach or followers, per post/channel.

- **Source:** native platform analytics (Instagram/TikTok/LinkedIn/X/YouTube), email
  (open/click), community (replies).
- **Benchmark (organic, varies by platform & follower count):** Instagram 1–3%, TikTok
  4–8%+, LinkedIn 2–4%, X ~0.5–1%. Smaller accounts usually run higher %.
- **Read:** the baseline pulse. Flat reach with rising engagement % = content resonates;
  high reach with low engagement = it's seen, not felt.

### Comments (depth of engagement)
Comments are worth more than likes — they cost effort and signal real reaction. Read volume
*and* substance: "🔥" is weak; "this is exactly the problem I have at work" is strong.

- **Source:** platform; **`last30days`** for comment text and sentiment across platforms.
- **Read:** the *content* of comments is qualitative gold — it tells you what actually
  resonates and in whose words. Mine it for the next message.

### Share Rate
Of people who see content, the share who pass it on (shares/reposts/sends ÷ reach).

- **Source:** platform share counts; referral/share events in product.
- **Benchmark:** anything ≥1–2% organic share rate is strong; shares are the rarest, highest-
  signal action because the sharer stakes *their own* reputation on you.
- **Read:** the purest resonance metric — people only share what says something about *them*.
  Low share rate = the brand isn't identity-expressing or useful-enough-to-pass-on.

### UGC count (user-generated content)
Volume of content *users* make about the brand unprompted — reviews, posts, videos, memes,
templates, fan art, integrations.

- **Source:** social listening, branded-hashtag tracking, review platforms.
- **Read:** UGC is resonance compounding into a moat — it's awareness *and* advocacy
  generated by users. Near-zero unprompted UGC after meaningful reach = the brand is used, not
  loved.

## Diagnosing low Resonance

- **High reach, low engagement/share** → the content informs but doesn't move. It's not
  emotional, surprising, useful-to-pass-on, or identity-expressing. Likely too feature-led,
  too corporate, or too safe.
- **Good engagement, low share/UGC** → people enjoy it but won't *attach their name* to it.
  The brand lacks a point of view or an identity worth signaling. This caps Advocacy.
- **Resonance high, Conversion low** → you've built an audience, not a business — common for
  content brands. Tighten the path from feeling to action.

## Levers (only if Resonance is the bottleneck)

- **A point of view** — brands that stand for something get shared; neutral ones get scrolled.
  Take a stance the ICP already feels.
- **Make the customer the hero** — UGC, testimonials, and "made with X" surfaces give users a
  reason and a way to spread it.
- **Identity-expressing artifacts** — branded results, badges, year-in-review, shareable
  outputs: things users *want* to post because it makes *them* look good.
- **Speak human** — one consistent, distinctive voice (see the voice notes below) beats polished
  corporate copy for emotional connection.

## Voice & tone — the qualitative half of resonance

When metrics are thin, score voice directly. A resonant brand voice is **consistent**
(same personality across ad, app, error message, support reply), **distinctive** (you could
tell it's them with the logo covered), and **audience-matched** (talks the way the ICP talks).
Red flags: tone whiplash between surfaces, generic "we're passionate about empowering" filler,
features where a feeling should be.

## Common ways the number lies

- **Vanity likes** — likes are cheap; weight comments, saves, and shares far higher.
- **Engagement bait** — "tag a friend" inflates engagement without real resonance; check if it
  produces UGC or repeat attention, not just one interaction.
- **Borrowed virality** — a meme/trend can spike a post without building *brand* resonance;
  look for whether new attention remembers the brand a week later.
