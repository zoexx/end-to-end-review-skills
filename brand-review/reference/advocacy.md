# Advocacy (传播) — do they bring others?

The bottom of the funnel that feeds the top. Advocacy is whether customers become a *growth
engine* — recommending, referring, repurchasing, and talking about you unprompted. It's what
turns a linear funnel into a compounding loop: advocacy lowers blended CAC, lifts LTV, and
manufactures awareness for free. The question: **would they recommend you, and do they
actually do it?**

A brand can score 🟢 on every stage above and still cap out here — and a brand strong in
advocacy can tolerate weaker upstream stages because the loop compounds. This is the highest-
leverage stage when it's working and the most-ignored when it isn't.

## Metrics

### NPS (Net Promoter Score)
"How likely are you to recommend us?" 0–10. `% promoters (9–10) − % detractors (0–6)`. Range
−100 to +100.

- **Source:** in-product or post-purchase survey (Delighted, in-app, email).
- **Benchmark:** > 0 means more promoters than detractors; **≥ 30 is good, ≥ 50 is excellent,
  ≥ 70 is world-class.** Compare to your category — software medians sit ~30–40.
- **Watch:** NPS measures *stated* intent. Pair it with referral rate (revealed behavior) —
  high NPS + low referral rate means they *would* recommend but you've given them no easy way.

### Referral Rate
Share of new customers who came from an existing customer's recommendation/referral.

- **Source:** referral-program attribution, "how did you hear about us?", invite tracking.
- **Read:** the revealed-preference counterpart to NPS. Even a modest organic referral rate
  (say 15–30% of new customers) dramatically lowers blended CAC. Near-zero referral with high
  NPS = a missing *mechanism*, not missing goodwill.

### Repeat Purchase / Retention
Share of customers who buy again / stay subscribed; cohort retention curves.

- **Source:** billing/CRM cohort analysis.
- **Benchmark:** varies by model, but the shape matters — a retention curve that *flattens*
  (a stable base that keeps using you) is the signature of fit; one that decays to zero means
  no durable value, which also kills LTV upstream in Conversion.
- **Read:** repeat purchase is advocacy toward *yourself* — the precondition for advocacy to
  others. People don't refer what they don't keep using.

### Brand Mention
Unprompted positive mentions in the wild — reviews, social, word-of-mouth, "we use X" posts.

- **Source:** social listening, review platforms, **`last30days`** for fresh mention volume +
  sentiment.
- **Read:** the public face of advocacy. Rising positive mention is the loop feeding awareness;
  rising *negative* mention is detractors amplifying — treat as urgent.

## Diagnosing low Advocacy

- **High NPS, low referral rate** → goodwill exists, the *mechanism* doesn't. Add an easy,
  rewarding way to refer; ask at the moment of delight.
- **Low NPS, low repeat** → the product doesn't deliver enough value to advocate for. This is
  not a referral-program problem; it's upstream (Conversion's retention leak / the core value).
  Don't paper over a leaky product with referral incentives — you'll just spread disappointment
  faster.
- **Good repeat, low public mention** → users stay but stay quiet. They lack a *reason or
  artifact* to talk (overlaps Resonance — give them something identity-expressing to share).
- **Strong advocacy everywhere** → 🌟 this is a moat. Quantify the blended-CAC reduction it
  buys and protect it; it's the cheapest growth you have.

## Levers (only if Advocacy is the bottleneck)

- **Build the referral mechanism** — a frictionless, mutually-rewarding referral/invite flow,
  triggered at peak satisfaction (after a win, not at signup).
- **Close the NPS loop** — route detractors to support/recovery, route promoters to a referral
  or review ask. Acting on NPS, not just measuring it, is what moves it.
- **Manufacture talkability** — shareable results, milestones, and "made with X" artifacts give
  happy users a reason and a way to spread the word (shared lever with Resonance).
- **Make retention the product team's metric** — repeat purchase is the foundation; without it
  there is no one to advocate.

## Common ways the number lies

- **NPS sampling bias** — surveying only active power users inflates it; include the disengaged.
- **Incentivized reviews/referrals** — paid-for mentions aren't advocacy; weight organic far
  higher and watch sentiment, not just volume.
- **Survivorship** — repeat-purchase % among *remaining* customers ignores everyone who already
  churned; always read it as a cohort curve, not a single rate.
